The Wayfall

The Wayfall is not a world.
It is what remains of a path.

Long before its collapse, this space existed only as a stabilizing layer — a hidden stratum that waystones passed through when carrying travelers across vast distances. It was never meant to be seen, walked, or settled. Each teleportation carved stress into it, each jump shaving away stability like stone ground down by centuries of footsteps.

Eventually, the way broke.

Now the Wayfall is a crumbling void of fractured stone routes, suspended over endless depth. Chunks of road, pillars, and half-formed terrain drift where solid passage once existed. Gravity is unreliable. The ground gives way without warning. What looks like safety is often a lie.

There is no sky — only a dim, directionless gloom lit by the fading glow of waystone energy. Runes hang half-burned into stone, flickering as if remembering their original purpose. Some still hum faintly, trying to complete a journey that will never finish.

Stone dominates everything, but it is wrong stone:
cracked, eroded, hollowed by transit. Massive fault lines split the land open, revealing nothing beneath. Dust drifts upward instead of falling. Debris slowly descends forever, never reaching a bottom.

Creatures here are not native in the traditional sense. They are accidents — stone warped by passage, guardians left behind when their stations fell, and mimics that learned to imitate the one thing travelers still trust: the waystone.

Travelers who enter the Wayfall do not arrive cleanly. They fall. Some reappear far from where they intended to go. Others never leave the dimension at all, becoming part of its ruins — marked in cairns, statues, and silent monuments scattered across the broken routes.

The Wayfall does not hate you.
It does not hunt you.

It simply no longer knows how to be a path.

# Cephalari Species Codex (Wayfall Stranded Variant)

## 1. Forgotten Origins
The Cephalari are a sapient nautiloid-descended species from an unknown dimension. Their arrival in Wayfall was the result of a catastrophic dimensional rupture that destroyed their memory-shell archives and severed all knowledge of their home.

Only instinctive fragments remain:
- Spiral motifs in their art and engineering
- A biological expectation of buoyancy
- Pressure-based logic instincts
- A cultural ache for a home they cannot name

They are a people defined by amnesia, adaptation, and survival.

## 2. Physiology in Wayfall
Cephalari bodies were never meant for land. Their original biology relied on drifting and buoyancy, not walking.

### 2.1 Morphology
- A spiral shell reinforced with Wayfall crystalline dust
- 20–40 tentacles capable of both fine manipulation and rigid bracing
- Internal pressure sacs that maintain body shape in shifting gravity
- Chromatophores that glow brightly in air
- A blink-reflex that lets them dodge short distances

### 2.2 Environmental Needs
- No longer require water
- Thrive only in Wayfall’s atmosphere
- Suffocate slowly in overworld-type air
- Immune to fall damage due to pressure sacs and blink reflex

### 2.3 Lifespan
- 180–260 years
- Memory degradation is common due to dimensional trauma

## 3. Mount-Bound Biology
Cephalari cannot move efficiently on their own. Their bodies are adapted for drifting, not terrestrial locomotion. To survive in Wayfall, they rely on scavenged-tech mobility mounts—machines built from fragments of their forgotten dimension and Wayfall debris.

Mount Rules:
- Every **adult** Cephalari spawns with one mount
- Cephalari mounts despawn when the Cephalari despawns
- Mounts are treated as extensions of the Cephalari body
- Each mount type reflects a different survival strategy

## 4. The Four Scavenged-Tech Mounts

### 4.1 The Spiral Strider
A multi-legged walker built from shell fragments and salvaged pistons.
- Role: height, stability, terrain mastery
- Movement: slow, extremely stable, immune to knockback
- Silhouette: tall stilt-walker with spiral-pattern legs
- Origin: reconstructed from ancestral shell plates and dimensional scrap
- Symbolism: continuity and the spiral memory of their lost home

### 4.2 The Drift Skimmer
A hovering manta-shaped craft that glides using lightweight membrane wings and pressure-vent propulsion.
- Role: speed, long-distance traversal
- Movement: smooth gliding, short vertical bursts
- Silhouette: flat ray-like chassis with glowing vents
- Origin: teleportation plate shards + flexible membrane wings
- Symbolism: echoes their aquatic ancestry without requiring water

### 4.3 The Treadwinder Chassis
A compact, armored mobility rig built on spiral-linked tank treads.
- Role: everyday mobility, rugged terrain
- Movement: smooth rolling, zero-turn pivot, short hop boost
- Silhouette: low chassis with dual spiral treads and a shell-cradle seat
- Origin: memory-shell fragments + gyroscopic stabilizers + coral-tread loops
- Symbolism: resilience, ingenuity, and the Cephalari’s refusal to be immobilized

This is the default and most iconic Cephalari mount.

### 4.4 The Echo Harness
A semi-organic exosuit that the Cephalari “slots into,” turning them into a tall, armored walker.
- Role: combat, defense
- Movement: balanced stride, blink-dash, high jumps
- Silhouette: four-limbed armored walker with shell plating
- Origin: resonance engines + reinforced shell plates
- Symbolism: a fusion of Cephalari identity and survival instinct

## 5. Society and Culture

### 5.1 Tide-Kin Clusters
Cephalari form kin groups based on:
- Shell pattern motifs
- Mount type
- Shared memory fragments

These clusters act as families, guilds, and survival units.

### 5.2 Elder Spirals
Elders serve as memory anchors, preserving what little remains of pre-Wayfall history.

### 5.3 Values
- Adaptation
- Continuity
- Symbiosis with mounts
- Stability in a hostile dimension

## 6. Wayfall Villages
Cephalari villages are rumored to exist only in the Wayfall dimension.

Note: village worldgen is lore-only right now.

### 6.1 Architecture
- Spiral platforms suspended by rope-like coral fibers
- Bio-ceramic structures fused with Wayfall crystal
- Stabilized chambers to counter shifting gravity
- Memory Gardens grown from Wayfall flora
- Mount platforms for each Cephalari

### 6.2 Village Layout
- Central Spiral Hall
- Ceramic Kilns
- Flow Engine Chambers
- Crystal Beds
- Scent Archives
- Mount Bays

## 7. Professions
Cephalari professions revolve around stabilizing Wayfall and maintaining their mounts.
- Ceramist — crafts bio-ceramic blocks
- Flowwright — builds pressure-logic machinery
- Crystal Gardener — cultivates Wayfall flora
- Shellsmith — creates shell armor and spiral shields
- Archivist — preserves scent-encoded memories
- Navigator — maps shifting terrain

## 8. Threats and Predators
Wayfall is hostile, and Cephalari villages face constant danger.
- Wraithlike distortive entities
- Riftborn Cephalari (corrupted subspecies)
- Gravity-adapted predators
- Spatial instability events

Mounts are essential for survival.

## 9. Subspecies
Three known variants exist:
- Sky-Pelagic Cephalari — gliders, bright shells
- Deepfall Cephalari — bioluminescent, distortion-resistant
- Riftborn Cephalari — corrupted, hostile, red-gold shells

## 10. Zombification Rules

### 10.1 Zombified Cephalari
When a Cephalari is zombified:
- Their original mount does NOT appear
- No separate zombie-type mount entity is spawned during conversion
- Babies can spawn as jockeys on a small, random vanilla mount

Note: earlier design drafts included a “random zombie-type mount” system for zombified Cephalari.
The current implementation does **not** spawn a zombie-type mount entity.

### 10.2 Conversion Back to Cephalari
When a zombified Cephalari is cured:
- The Cephalari returns to normal
- Their zombie mount is removed and replaced with the appropriate Cephalari mount type
- Zoglins are excluded and cannot convert; if the zombie mount was a Zoglin, it is removed and replaced with a random Cephalari mount

This prevents impossible conversions and keeps the mount system consistent.

## 11. Player Interaction
Players can:
- Trade for Wayfall-exclusive materials
- Learn pressure-logic machinery
- Acquire shell-based armor
- Protect villages from Riftborn raids
- Use Cephalari maps to navigate unstable terrain

Cephalari villages act as safe havens in an unpredictable dimension.
